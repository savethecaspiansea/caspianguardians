About Caspian Guardians

Protecting the Future of the Caspian Sea

Caspian Guardians is a youth-driven environmental initiative focused on raising awareness, sharing knowledge, and inspiring action to protect one of the world’s most unique ecosystems.

Mission

Our Mission

Our mission is to help protect the Caspian Sea by raising environmental awareness, promoting responsible action, and encouraging communities to care about the future of this vital natural ecosystem.

We aim to inform people, especially young generations, about the challenges facing the Caspian Sea and inspire meaningful involvement in its preservation.

Vision

A future where the Caspian Sea is cleaner, healthier, and protected through education, collaboration, and sustainable action.

What We Do

How We Contribute

We combine research, digital content, and public engagement to make environmental issues easier to understand and harder to ignore.

Environmental Awareness

We create accessible content that helps people understand the ecological problems affecting the Caspian Sea.

Research & Information

We collect, review, and organize environmental information to support educational and awareness-based initiatives.

Community Engagement

We encourage students, young people, and communities to take interest in environmental responsibility and local action.

  • Sharing educational materials about the Caspian Sea
  • Highlighting environmental risks and key facts
  • Promoting eco-friendly values and responsible habits
  • Using media and digital platforms to spread awareness

Why It Matters

Why the Caspian Sea Matters

#1

Largest of Its Kind

The Caspian Sea is the world’s largest inland body of water, making it one of the most geographically and environmentally significant water ecosystems on Earth.

5

Shared by Five Countries

Azerbaijan, Kazakhstan, Turkmenistan, Iran, and Russia all depend on the Caspian, which makes its protection a regional and international responsibility.

!

Under Pressure

Pollution, biodiversity loss, and environmental change continue to threaten the sea’s ecosystems, habitats, and long-term health.
